This is the first post on the SentinelHunt blog.

The plan is to use this space for longer-form content that doesn't fit
neatly into a hunt entry — walkthroughs of how a hunt was developed, notes
from real investigations, and general thinking on detection engineering and
threat hunting with Microsoft Sentinel.

## What to expect

- Hunt development write-ups
- Investigation case studies (anonymised)
- Tooling and methodology notes

Each post lives as its own Markdown file under `content/blog/`, with a
`published` flag in the frontmatter to control whether it appears on the
site. Setting `published: false` keeps a draft in the repo without showing
it to visitors.
