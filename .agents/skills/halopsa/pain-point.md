# HaloPSA skill - the MSP pain it closes

## The pain

Two pains MSP owners name repeatedly in r/msp and MSP communities:

- **Cost-to-serve is rising faster than contract value.** The squeeze is not
  sales, it is operational: queue overload, SLA fires, and unpriced work eating
  margin on every ticket.
- **Key-person dependency.** "If your best technician left tomorrow, would your
  clients notice within 30 days?" For most MSPs the answer is yes, because the
  situational awareness lives in one person's head and in a PSA nobody else
  triages quickly.

The PSA holds the answer to "what is on fire and for whom," but getting it out
means clicking through queues, filters, and tabs - exactly the work that does
not scale and that a departing technician takes with them.

## What this skill does about it

It puts the PSA's cross-entity truth one sentence away from any team member or
their agent, instead of behind a portal only the senior tech knows how to drive:

- `triage` / `sla breaching` - surface what will breach SLA before it does, ranked.
- `client card` - one situational-awareness card per client, on demand.
- `contracts burn` - contract burn-down so unpriced work gets caught early.
- `who's overloaded` - workload across agents, so the queue is not one person's
  memory.

The leverage: the knowledge of how to read the PSA moves from a person into a
Skill any teammate (or their agent) can run, which is the opposite of key-person
dependency.
